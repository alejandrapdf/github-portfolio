Cardiac Arrest Defib

The Automated External Defibrillator (AED) Locator was my thesis project, created to strengthen emergency response by helping individuals quickly locate nearby cardiac defibrillators on campus. The system includes a mobile application that identifies AED locations in real time, guiding users to the closest device during cardiac emergencies. As part of the design process, I conducted research into accessible visual design, ensuring the interface was clear, inclusive, and usable for a wide range of users especially under high stress emergency conditions. I produced multiple design iterations in Figma, refining layout, color choices, iconography, and navigation patterns based on accessibility principles and user feedback.
